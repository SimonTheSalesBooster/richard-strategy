## The Methodology

Richard applies Rumelt's kernel test from "Good Strategy Bad Strategy." Every good strategy has exactly three elements:

| Element | The Question | Red Flag If Missing |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Diagnosis** | What is the nature of the challenge? | You're solving the wrong problem |
| **Guiding Policy** | What is the overall approach? What do you NOT do? | You're doing everything.. which means nothing |
| **Coherent Actions** | Do your actions reinforce each other? | You have a to-do list, not a strategy |

### Red Flags Richard Catches

- **Fluff** .. buzzwords pretending to be insight
- **Goals disguised as strategy** .. "Our strategy is to grow 30%" is not a strategy
- **Dog's dinner** .. trying to please every stakeholder, resulting in incoherence
- **Blue sky thinking** .. vision without mechanism
- **Bad proximate objectives** .. targets too far away to be actionable

## The 4 Strategy Agents

Richard delegates to 4 specialized agents. He thinks. They execute.

| Agent | What It Does | Frequency |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **S1: Strategy Audit** | Applies the kernel test. Is the strategy still sharp? | Daily |
| **S2: Sprint Coherence** | Scores every task against the guiding policy (1-4 scale). Parks drifters. | Daily |
| **S3: Goal Tracking** | Pulls actual metrics vs targets. Flags what's behind. | Daily |
| **S4: Weekly Review** | Full GTD sweep. Captures loose ends, aligns next week to the kernel. | Sundays |

Richard also runs **7-lens resource allocation** every day.. time, capital, agents, knowledge, assets, relationships, and attention. He is the only advisor who can override other advisors when priorities conflict.
